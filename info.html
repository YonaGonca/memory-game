How to Play?

Log in or Sign up:

  1. If you already have an account, log in using your credentials.
  2. If you don’t have an account yet, sign up by providing the required details, such as username, password, and contact information.

Option Selection:

  1. After logging in, you will be redirected to a page where you can select your game preferences.
  2. You can choose to play alone or add more players. If you decide to add more players, make sure they are also registered beforehand. Additional players must provide their email and password to be added to the game.
  3. You can also customize the card style and board size according to your preferences.
  4. There are options to enable trio mode and time-attack mode, with various difficulty levels available for time-attack mode.
  5. After selecting your desired options, press the "Start" button to begin the game.

Playing

  1. The cards are randomly arranged on the previously selected board.
  2. Choose the card number in the "Choose a card" text box and click "Flip" to see what's underneath.
  3. Look for two cards with the same symbol. Pay attention to the images on each card.
  4. If you find a pair, the cards are removed from the game, and you earn a point. If they don't match, the cards are flipped back. Remember the flipped cards to try matching them later.
  5. The game ends when all cards are removed.
  6. You can check your statistics after the game and compare your results with your friends.
  7. Play again to get a lower time or try other game modes for different experiences! Have fun exploring all the options and challenging your skills in different modes.

Game Modes

Solo Mode

In this mode, you play alone with no time limit, but the goal is to complete the game as quickly as possible. You can practice your memory and concentration without the pressure of a countdown, focusing on finding all card pairs as fast as possible. Try to beat your own record each time!

Multiplayer Mode

In this mode, you can play against friends or family. Each player takes turns trying to find matching pairs, and the player with the most pairs at the end wins. It's a great option to spend time with friends and compete in a fun way!

Countdown Mode

In this mode, you have a limited time to find all card pairs. This mode offers various difficulty levels, allowing you to choose your preferred challenge. You can select different time limits, from easier to harder, to test your memory and agility. The goal is to complete the game before time runs out, overcoming challenges as you increase the difficulty.

Trio Mode

In this mode, instead of finding pairs, you must find sets of three cards with the same symbol. It’s an interesting twist on the classic memory game and adds a new level of difficulty and strategy. Try it if you're looking for a different challenge!

Fun Facts:

Origin

Memory games have an ancient origin, with records of early versions being played in ancient Egypt, China, and India. The modern game, as we know it today, was popularized in Europe in the 19th century, especially in Germany, where it appeared in children's activity books. Since then, it has become a popular pastime and an educational tool worldwide.

Psychology

The Swiss psychologist Carl Jung used a version of the memory game in his therapy sessions. He believed that the game helped access the unconscious and reveal thought patterns. Thus, the game became not only a therapeutic tool but also a way to explore the human mind.

Benefits

Studies suggest that playing memory games regularly can help improve not only memory but also other cognitive skills, such as concentration, logical reasoning, and decision-making, making it a valuable activity for all ages.

Music

Did you know that listening to Mozart while playing a memory game can temporarily boost cognitive performance and memory capacity? This phenomenon, known as the 'Mozart Effect,' can be an interesting way to make your memory game even more effective!
